Cloud CRM

How the System Works & How Sales Use It

Report 1 — One place · your voice · cloud

At a glance

Cadence is the only CRM — no copy from, import to, or sync with other CRMs. Everything stays in Cadence; runs in the cloud; access from anywhere; no installs.

  • What: Cloud CRM · your voice · leads · deals · contacts · tasks · activities
  • Flow: Sign in → create or join an organization → open an organization → work in that org’s CRM (company + tone optional per org)
  • Who: Sales · you can be in several orgs; in each org you work as one salesperson with that org’s data
  • Best for: Teams that want one place in the cloud for copy + pipeline · no external CRM

Intended flow (target vision)

After login you create an organization or join one (e.g. accept an invite). When you open an organization, you go to that org’s CRM — one salesperson with full access to that org’s data. Each org has an owner who can accept/reject join requests and invite others by email.

  • Only this system — No copy from another CRM; no import or send to another CRM. Cadence handles everything.
  • Different orgs — You can join several organizations and switch by opening the org you want.
  • Today (current UI) — Backend supports organizations and org-scoped data; the frontend does not yet show create/join org or org switcher, so in the UI you still see a single account and your own data. When the frontend org flow is built, the flow above will apply.

Three jobs

The system does three things for you: writes copy in your voice, stores all your sales data, and lets you attach copy to a contact or deal and reuse it from History.

  1. Writes — Cadence generates sales copy in your voice. You pick a type (Sales Email, Follow-up, CRM Note, Deal Message, Workflow), a goal (e.g. schedule meeting, follow up after demo, close the deal), and your org’s brand tone. The built-in copy engine produces ready-to-send text so you spend less time drafting and more time selling.
  2. Holds — All your sales data lives in one place. You add leads and convert them into contacts and deals without re-typing. The pipeline shows every deal by stage; tasks and activities keep follow-ups and call/meeting/email logs in order. No spreadsheets, no other CRM — everything stays in Cadence.
  3. Attaches — Every piece of copy you send is linked to a contact or deal and saved in History. You can copy to clipboard, regenerate with fresh context, or send again to another contact or deal. Nothing is lost; you reuse and refine instead of starting from scratch.

One CRM. Cloud. No installs.

Homepage

Business reason: First impression and clear entry points matter. The homepage tells visitors what Cadence is and gives one-click paths to sign in, get started, or try the app — so prospects and returning users know where to go without confusion.

What this page offers:

  • Value message — What the product is: cloud CRM, your voice, one place for copy and pipeline.
  • Sign In / Get started — Direct link to Login for returning users or new sign-ups.
  • Try the app — Sandbox access so anyone can explore without creating an account.
  • Help, Privacy, Terms — Links for support and legal pages.

Login

Business reason: Secure access is the foundation of trust. Sales teams need a single sign-in that works from any device, protects company data, and lets new users try the product without committing. Login is the gate to the only CRM they use — no separate tools or credentials.

What this page offers:

  • Sign up — Create account (name, email, password) so new reps can join the org or start their own.
  • Sign in — Email + password; optional 2FA (6-digit code) for extra security on shared devices.
  • Try the app — Sandbox mode with sample data so prospects can explore the CRM and copy flow without saving anything to an account.

Organizations

Business reason: Real sales happens in teams and per client or division. Organizations let one person belong to multiple workspaces (e.g. “Acme Corp” and “Client X”) and switch context without mixing data. Owners control who joins and who gets invited — so pipeline and copy stay scoped to the right team.

What this page offers:

  • Create organization — Start a new team workspace (company or client); you become the owner.
  • Join organization — Accept an invite or have a join request accepted; then you see that org’s data when you open it.
  • Open organization — Switch into an org to work in its CRM (leads, deals, contacts, copy) as one salesperson with full access to that org’s data only.

Onboarding

Business reason: Generated copy must sound like the company, not generic. One-time setup of company name and brand tone (Professional / Friendly / Persuasive) ensures every email, follow-up, and deal message is on-brand from day one — no per-rep guesswork.

What this page offers:

  • Company name — Used in generated copy (e.g. “how [Your Company] can help…”). Set once per org.
  • Brand tone — Professional (formal, concise), Friendly (warm, approachable), or Persuasive (confident, action-oriented). Applied to all generated copy.
  • Save & Continue — Lands you on the Dashboard; you can change these later in Settings.

Dashboard

Business reason: Sales reps need one place to start their day: see pipeline health, then generate the right message without jumping between tools. The Dashboard is the hub — copy creation and key metrics in one view so time goes to selling, not searching.

What this page offers:

  • Generate copy — Pick type (Sales Email, Follow-up, CRM Note, Deal Message, Workflow), goal (e.g. schedule meeting, close the deal), optional context and length → Generate copy → Generated Copy page.
  • Stats — Generated this week, Templates count, Sent to CRM; active leads, active deals, pipeline value, deals won/lost.
  • Recent activity — Last 5 items sent to CRM; “View full history” goes to History.

Generated Copy

Business reason: First drafts are rarely perfect. This page is where copy becomes send-ready: edit, tweak tone or length, or regenerate without losing context. It bridges “created” and “sent” so reps stay in flow and don’t paste into external tools.

What this page offers:

  • Edit — Read and change the draft before sending.
  • Copy — Copy full text to clipboard for use in email clients or other apps.
  • Adjust Copy — Make shorter, friendlier, or more persuasive without regenerating from scratch.
  • Regenerate — New draft with same type/goal/context.
  • Send to CRM — Attach this copy to a contact or deal and save it in History.

Send to CRM

Business reason: Copy only creates value when it’s tied to the right person or deal. “Send to CRM” means “save and attach in Cadence” — no export to another CRM. Every sent piece is stored and reusable, so follow-ups and reuse are one click away.

What this page offers:

  • Contact or Deal — Choose whether this copy is for a person (contact) or an opportunity (deal).
  • Search and select — Find the contact or deal by name; one selection per send.
  • Confirm & Send — Saves the copy in History linked to that contact or deal.
  • After send — Create Another (back to Dashboard) or View History.

Leads

Business reason: New interest (website, referral, event) must be captured before it goes cold. Leads are the funnel before pipeline: one place to add, qualify, and convert into contacts and deals without re-typing. Conversion in one step keeps data clean and saves time.

What this page offers:

  • List and search — Name, email, company, status (New, Contacted, etc.), source (website, referral, etc.).
  • Add lead — Enter name, email, phone (optional), company (dropdown), source, status.
  • Edit / Delete — Update or remove; delete is confirmed.
  • Convert — Turn a lead into a contact and/or a deal in one step; name, email, phone, company are carried over so you don’t re-enter data.

Pipeline (Deals)

Business reason: Revenue is forecast by stage. A visual pipeline (Kanban) shows every opportunity, its value, and stage so reps and managers can prioritize and move deals toward close. Won/Lost is clear so the Dashboard and reporting stay accurate.

What this page offers:

  • Kanban view — Columns: Qualification → Proposal → Negotiation → Closed Won / Closed Lost; each deal is a card with name, value, stage dropdown.
  • New deal — Name, value, stage, expected close date (optional), company.
  • Move stage — Use dropdown on card; moving to Closed Won or Closed Lost marks the deal accordingly (counts on Dashboard).
  • Edit / Delete — Update details or remove; delete is confirmed.

Tasks

Business reason: Follow-ups and next steps get dropped when they’re only in email or memory. Tasks give reps a single list with due dates and optional link to a lead or deal, so nothing slips and activity stays tied to the right opportunity.

What this page offers:

  • List — Title, due date, optional link to lead or deal; checkbox to mark complete.
  • Filter — All / Pending / Overdue so reps can focus on what’s due.
  • Add task — Title, description, due date; optionally link to lead or deal.
  • Edit / Complete — Change details or check off when done.

Activities

Business reason: Calls, meetings, and emails need a shared record so the team and future-you know what happened with whom and on which deal. Activities are the timeline — no scattered notes or separate tools.

What this page offers:

  • Log activity — Type (call, meeting, email, note), subject, body; optionally link to a contact or deal.
  • View and filter — All, or by contact, or by deal — so you see a full timeline per person or per opportunity.
  • List — Type, subject, body, date for every logged item.

Contacts

Business reason: People are the unit of outreach and relationship. Contacts are the single record per person: used when sending copy to CRM and when logging activities, so every message and call is attached to the right name and company.

What this page offers:

  • List and search — Name, email, phone, company; search by name, email, or phone.
  • Add contact — Name, email, phone, company (dropdown).
  • Edit — Update any field. Contacts appear in Send to CRM and in activity filters.

Companies

Business reason: Deals and leads belong to accounts, not just people. Companies are the account list — one place to maintain account names so leads and deals can be linked consistently for reporting and filtering (e.g. “all deals at Acme”).

What this page offers:

  • List and search — All companies (accounts) you sell to.
  • Add company — Create a new account name.
  • Edit — Update name. Companies appear in the company dropdown when creating or editing leads and deals.

Templates

Business reason: Repeating situations (first contact, post-demo follow-up, closing) should not mean re-picking type and goal every time. Templates are pre-set type + goal combos so reps go from “I need a follow-up after demo” to one click and generate — less friction, more consistency.

What this page offers:

  • Template list — e.g. “First contact email”, “Follow-up after meeting”, “Demo reminder”, “Closing deal message”, “Re-engagement email”.
  • Use template — Opens the Dashboard with that template’s type and goal already selected so you can add context and generate immediately.

History

Business reason: Sent copy is an asset. History keeps every piece linked to a contact or deal so reps can reuse, tweak, or send again to someone else without re-generating. No copy is lost; everything is searchable and actionable.

What this page offers:

  • List — Type, copy snippet, recipient name, recipient type (contact/deal), date.
  • Search — Filter by copy text, type, or recipient name.
  • Copy — Copy full text to clipboard.
  • Regenerate — Go to Dashboard with that copy’s context to create a fresh version.
  • Send again — Go to Send to CRM with that copy to send it to another contact or deal.

Settings

Business reason: Brand and security must be manageable without leaving the app. Settings centralize company name and tone, org switching, (for owners) invites and join requests, 2FA, and account actions so the team stays in control of identity and safety.

What this page offers:

  • Brand — Change company name and brand tone; Save Changes.
  • Organization — Switch current org; manage organizations. Owner: invite by email, accept/reject join requests.
  • 2FA — Enable (QR or secret + 6-digit code) or disable (password + code).
  • Logout / Delete account — Sign out or permanently delete account (with confirmation).

Help · Privacy · Terms
