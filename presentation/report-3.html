Comparison With Top CRMs

Report 3 — Us vs them · when to choose

In brief

Cadence is a cloud CRM that writes sales copy in your brand voice and keeps leads, deals, contacts, companies, tasks, and activities in one place. Top CRMs are full platforms with teams, roles, integrations, and enterprise features. This report compares what Cadence has vs what they typically have.

  • Cadence: Cloud CRM · your voice · leads · deals · contacts · tasks · one place · access from any browser
  • Top CRMs: Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive, Zoho, Monday — full sales + marketing + service · teams · roles · integrations
  • Strengths: Sales copy (5 types, 6 goals, tone) · convert lead to contact/deal · pipeline · tasks · activities · copy history · cloud
  • Gaps vs top CRMs: No teams/roles · no lead assignment or scoring · no contact–deal link or “last activity” · no email/calendar/phone integrations · no custom reports or manager dashboards · no SSO/audit/compliance

Cadence focus

We focus on writing sales copy (type + goal + tone) and holding core sales data: leads, pipeline, contacts, companies, tasks, activities. All in the cloud, no installs.

  • Copy — type + goal + tone · generate → send · History
  • Data — leads · pipeline · contacts · companies · convert
  • Cloud — any browser · no installs

Them = full stack. Us = copy + pipeline.

When to choose

Choose Cadence when you want sales copy in your brand voice and core CRM in one place; choose an enterprise CRM when you need roles, teams, integrations, or enterprise reporting.

Choose Cadence when: Sales copy in your voice and core CRM in one place · cloud (or optional self-host) · single user or small team · convert, pipeline, dashboard, copy history — and you don’t need lead scoring, team/manager views, or heavy integrations.

Choose enterprise CRM when: Roles (admin, manager, rep), teams, shared records, manager/team dashboards · integrations (email, calendar, telephony, ERP) or app marketplace · lead assignment, lead scoring, revenue forecasting, products on deals, custom reports, export · SSO/SAML, audit logs, compliance (e.g. GDPR, SOC 2).

Hybrid: Use Cadence for copy and send-to-CRM while using another system for team/organization. “Connect your CRM” can later integrate with external CRMs when that flow is built.

Takeaways

Cadence strengths: Cloud CRM — runs in the cloud; access from any browser; no installs. Sales copy generation with brand tone (5 types, 6 goals, templates); full lead/deal/contact/company/task/activity core; convert lead to contact/deal; simple pipeline and dashboard; copy history (regenerate, send again). Optional self-host for advanced users.

  • Gaps vs top CRMs: No teams or roles in UI (target vision: organizations; backend has org APIs) · no lead assignment or scoring (we have convert) · no contact–deal link or last-activity field in UI · no integrations or custom reporting · no SSO/audit/compliance
